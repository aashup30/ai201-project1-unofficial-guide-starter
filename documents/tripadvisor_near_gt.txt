TripAdvisor Forum: Good restaurants near Georgia Tech (Atlanta)
Source: https://www.tripadvisor.com/ShowTopic-g60898-i104-k14737543-Good_restaurants_near_Georgia_Tech-Atlanta_Georgia.html

Original question: Looking for good dinner spots within a 20 minute walk of Georgia Tech. Budget around $36 before tax and tip. Already considering South City Kitchen Midtown and Antico's Pizza.

---

Post 1 (Scott W):
Wagaya on 14th Street is one of my family's favorite Japanese places and they are good quality for the price. Antico's is Neapolitan-style pizza and admittedly very good, though not low-end. Blue India is another less-mentioned option — great for Indian food, especially if you're on the eastern side of campus near Peachtree Street.

---

Post 2 (bigbomoho, Destination Expert for Atlanta):
South City Kitchen is very good.

---

Post 3 (TransATL):
I had dinner the other night at Tabla (upscale Indian) and it was amazing. It's on 12th street and much closer to the north side of campus.

---

Post 4 (dlwinger, Destination Expert for Amelia Island):
You can take MARTA from the North Avenue station up a couple of stops to Midtown or Arts Center station and you will have tons of good restaurants to choose from. Ahn's is great for Vietnamese food. If you blow your budget one night, you can always make it up with a meal at the Waffle House or The Varsity.

---

Post 5 (kathscof):
Cypress Street Pint and Plate is a fun option — check to see if they still have the Sublime Donut burger. The food hall at Coda has good options. There are also fast food and fast casual places around 5th street where students get a lot of their quick food. Antico is delicious but not low-end and not a leisurely dining experience. Ahn's Kitchen on Peachtree Street is great for Vietnamese. Depends a lot on which side of campus you're on — west side limits your walkable options significantly.
